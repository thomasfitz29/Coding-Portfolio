The Oppurtunity

Fall 2020 semester I took CS307: "Intro To Software Engineering". We were tasked with creating a social media app with attributes from many major social media platorms.

The Solution

Our Team of 6 developed Boiler Comms, with myself as project lead. This social media app takes inspiration from many major social media sites. For example: the "wall" from facebook, "subreddits" and comments like reddit, and a character limit of twiter. We completed three seperant sprints throughout the semester, using the scrum methodolgy to set sprint goals. As project lead it was my job to designate the best tasks for each individual baised on their strengths. I was also a full stack developer primarily working on the backend/database design. The front end is built in android studio, and the backend in PHP/MySQL. Ultimatly we delivered a secure, fast, mobile app, albeit lacking in great aesthetic choices.

Objectives

  • Produce a social media site similar to that of other major social media sites

  • Preform 3 sprints throughout the semester making use of the scrum methodology

  • Leadership over 5 other developers

  • Demo and present the state of the app after each sprint

Reflection

  • Project Mangament: This was my first CS project leading a large team. There were times it was difficult, especially as team lead, like pulling all nighters before sprint due dates. However it was also rewarding getting teamates motivated and guiding them to improve. I learned a lot that will be applicable to my future work.
  • Importance of Front End Developers: Our team had two teammebers with limit front end development experience. The rest of our team were primarily backend developers up to this point. Developing an aesthetically pleasing UI proved more difficult than we imagined, and I was not fully happy with how the final product looked. Moving forward I will always remeber the importance of a strong frontend developer. This inspired me to take an app developemnt class.

I had a great time as full stack developer and team lead on this project. Though it is not my best work, I think it shows my development progression, when compared to my future projects.
