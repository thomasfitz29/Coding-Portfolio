Falling Weight Deflectometer Visualization Tool

Webapp designed to visualize and predict future road repairs.

Feb 2020 - Aug 2021

The Oppurtunity

The Indiana Department Of Transportation (INDOT) purchased multiple Falling Weight Deflectometers (FWD) for use by their road designers to predict road repairs. Unfortunatly this FWD device outputs comma seperated lists, barely readable to the average engineer.

The Solution

To remedy this we proposed and developed a solution in which a dashcam would be run simultaniously during the collection process then uploaded automnously to a server. This upload process linkis the dashcam footage geolocation metadata with the coresponding FWD geolocation metadata then preforms image extraction using ffmpeg and OpenCV to generate individual frames from the dashcam. These 4k images and coresponding FWD data are stored on an oracle database. Road Designers at INDOT can view these extracted images and more using our webapp. The webapp uses ArcGIS API and Google Maps API to display and color points on the map based on geolocation and FWD surface deflection respectlivly. Additionally road designers can enlarge images, view imagery driving into and leaving a testing location, and view FWD data as Text or Graphically. This system is currently used by INDOT road designers.

Objectives

  • Develop solution to replace manualy looking through FWD files

  • Develop an algrithm to best fit FWD data to GIS data

  • Demonstrate the use case and applications of the FWD visualization Tool

  • Ongoing support after product delivery

Gallary

Below is the initial view of the FWD Visualization Tool. The quality of a road is dictated by its surface deflection with a high surface deflection (datapoints colored red) indicating an unhealthy road. The scale can be seen in the bottom left corner. Clicking on colored datapoints will display an associated image. The user can navigate along the current road or select a new road using the control panel at the top of the page. Additional widgets can be displayed by clicking the buttons on the right side of the map.


To the left is a close up of the image displayed by clicking a datapoint. The image shows the road at the site of testing. Located underneath the image are a few buttons that preform various applications. The first icon (enlarge) expands the image to encompass 75% of the users screen. The next two icons change the image to display road imaegry extracted while driving to the current testing location and leaving the testing location. This gives the road designer engineers the options to see surrouning road imagery. The icon on the left displays additonal action buttons including an option to download a 4k version of the image.

Reflection

  • Frequently ask for feedback on designs from interested parties: Because I was working with a government agency there were a lot of regulations and security protocals to account for It was imperative that what I created aligned with the vision of the investment team but fit the security and aproval of the IT department. This required numerous redesigns to account for these concerns. In future projects I manage, I will work more directly with customer in the hopes this reduces the amount of redesigns.
  • Dont Loose Heart: A lot of the work in this project was backend algorithms for the extraction process then linking to geolcation metadata. The process of calculating the best guess fit of extraction images is a novel concept and at times proved extremely dificult. Through perserverence, determination, and a lot of trial and error, The alogrithm I created works in all but the most extreme cases (such as the driver going the wrong way for extended periods of time).

I had a great time as software development lead on this project. I got to work directly with the customer and their IT staff. Overall I learned a lot that is applicable to future products moving forward
